Trick Questions

The dark pattern you just encountered was trick questions. The trick questions dark pattern usually shows up when filling in a form/ signing up for a website. While filling in a form you respond to a question that tricks you into giving an answer you didn't intend. When glanced upon quickly the question appears to ask one thing, but when read carefully it asks another thing entirely. In the previous example, the thing you should pay attention to is the 3 checkboxes at the bottom of the form shown below.

The construction of the sentences is purposefully confusing and tries to trick users into subscribing to product offers. Also note how the boxes are already checked for you. If you do not want to sign up for any product offers, you would have to uncheck all the boxes. Leaving one box checked would result in you signing up for product offers. Where most people tend to mess up is they read through the options too quickly and don’t understand them or they don’t read them at all. Similar to the last example, it is your choice whether or not you want to sign up for the product offers. However, just make sure you know what you are signing up for, and if you don’t understand then call customer service and ask them for clarification. Click the next button to continue.
